The Ultimate Wedding Photography Guide for 2025 Couples

By Rajdhani Events • Updated 2025

Photos are the time machine of your wedding. Here’s how to plan your coverage, choose styles, and build a timeline so nothing gets missed.

Candid vs Traditional

  • Candid: real emotions, laughs, stolen glances—great for sangeet, haldi, and getting-ready moments.
  • Traditional: essential family portraits, rituals, stage greetings—organized and complete.

Drone & Film

  • Drone for venue reveals, baraat aerials, and sunset pheras (confirm venue permissions).
  • Cinematic highlights film (3–5 minutes) + full documentary cut (30–60 minutes).

Must-Have Shot List

  • Getting ready (bride/groom), first look, varmala, pheras, vidaai.
  • Parents and grandparents portraits; best friends; group shots by function.

Timeline Tips

Golden hour: plan couple portraits around sunrise/sunset for soft, flattering light.
Buffer time: keep 15–20 minutes between rituals for group photos.
